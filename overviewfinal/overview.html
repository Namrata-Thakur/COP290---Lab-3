Overview

 

"The Fault in Our Stars" is a novel by John Green that tells the story of two teenagers, Hazel Grace Lancaster and Augustus Waters, who meet at a cancer support group and fall in love. Hazel, who has terminal thyroid cancer, is initially reluctant to get close to Augustus because she doesn't want to hurt him when she eventually dies. However, they bond over their love of books and embark on a journey to meet Hazel's favorite author in Amsterdam.

During their trip, Hazel and Augustus face various challenges and setbacks, but their relationship grows stronger. However, Augustus' cancer returns, and he becomes very ill. In the end, he dies, leaving Hazel devastated but grateful for the time they had together. The book explores themes of love, loss, mortality, and the meaning of life.
